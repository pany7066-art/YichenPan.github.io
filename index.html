About

I am a third-year undergraduate majoring in Digital Media Technology at Shanghai University, currently on exchange at Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU), South Korea.

My research interests lie in 3D generative AI and cross-modal generation, with a strong belief in the scaling law as a guiding principle for advancing model capabilities.

Under the supervision of Prof. Mentian Li, I have hands-on experience spanning diffusion-based generative models, 3D Gaussian avatar reconstruction, and multimodal dataset construction.

I aim to bridge cutting-edge generative research with production-quality 3D content creation — making complex generative systems both technically rigorous and practically useful.

Script-to-Acting: Animating Characters with Context, Traits, and Emotion
Constructed a large-scale multimodal dataset of ~30 hours of theatrical footage. Designed a view-invariant 3D motion processing pipeline via GVHMR and HumanML3D protocols. Achieved cross-modal alignment across video, audio, and text for a context-sensitive autoregressive generation pipeline.

AvatarBrush: Monocular Reconstruction of Gaussian Avatars
Built an interactive editing system (Dear PyGui) around a disentangled Gaussian-Mesh 3D avatar representation with real-time keyframe animation, ROI selection, and texture stamping. Implemented zero-shot garment swapping. Validated on X-Human and ZJU-MoCap benchmarks with strong PSNR/SSIM metrics.
